A Ryokan is a traditional Japanese inn that typically features tatami-matted rooms, communal baths, and other public areas where visitors may wear nemaki and talk with the owner. Staying in a ryokan provides a more authentic Japanese experience than hotels. They are intimate, simple yet luxurious, and offer unrivaled hospitality

Here are some recommendations:

- Tawaraya in Kyoto

- Hiiragiya Ryokan in Kyoto

- Seikoro in Kyoto

- Ryokan Asakusa Shigetsu in Tokyo

- Asakusa Ryokan Toukaisou in Tokyo

A Capsule Hotel, also known as a pod hotel, is a type of hotel developed in Japan that features many small bed-sized rooms known as capsules. These hotels provide cheap, basic overnight accommodation for guests who do not require or who cannot afford larger, more expensive rooms

Advantages

- Extremely cheap

- Lots of community areas

- Convenient locations (They are usually located near train stations)

Disadvantages

- Less Privacy

- Little space which can trigger some people

A Guesthouse is a private home converted for guest accommodation, often family-owned. A Hostel is a budget-friendly lodging focusing on shared social experience, offering facilities like shared kitchens and social spaces, which offer facilities such as fully-equipped shared kitchens and dining areas, laundry rooms, social gathering spaces, etc

Recommended guesthouses

- Little Alaskan Guest House in Hakuba

- Guest House Kura in Suzaka

Hostels

- The Phat House in Hakuba

- Myoko Powder Hostel in Myoko

Staying in an Airbnb in Japan can provide a unique and authentic experience. It’s often cheaper than hotels, especially for groups, and offers the convenience of home-like amenities such as a kitchen and washing machine. It’s a great alternative in both urban and rural areas where traditional accommodations may be booked out or lacking

Advantages

- Cost-effective

- Local experience

- Amenities

Disadvantages

- Strict rules

- Depending on the host some Airbnb can be dirty or not have enough facilities

A Temple Stay is a cultural program in several Buddhist temples that allows participants to experience the life of Buddhist practitioners. It’s an opportunity to slow down, renew, and try meditative, spiritual practices such as sutra copying, meditation, and strolling the temple gardens. They are usually luxurious.

Here are some recommendations:

- Seichoji Temple in Kamogawa

- Mii-dera in Otsu

- Seikoro in Kyoto

- Zenkō-ji Temple in Nagano

- Ekoin Temple in Mount Koya

An Internet Cafe in Japan is a place that provides computer usage with internet access for a fee. They are not just for gaming, but also offer a variety of services including private booths with reclining chairs that can be rented on an hourly, daily or overnight rate.

Is it recommended?

- This makes them a cost-effective alternative to hotels, especially for those on a tight budget

- They are often used by people who need a place to stay for a few nights

- The cubicles are typically not lockable, which means your belongings might be at risk while you're away
